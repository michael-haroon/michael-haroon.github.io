&nbsp;

US and Israel struck Iran, thus affecting Iran's oil, gas, and petrochemical supply chain. The US does not trade with Iran in any significant manner, but China does. Anyone who depends on Iran or China for oil, gas, petrochemicals or anything closely related will be affected. The US gets most of its oil, gas, and petrochemical goods from Mexico and Canada, both of which produce their own oil, gas, and petrochemical goods from their land. I bet US prices will be affected only if arbitrageurs connect the two markets. Arbitrageurs will buy cheap here and sell expensive in Asia, thus pushing prices up in the States, while meeting demand in Asia by providing liquidity.

&nbsp;

That is the bad thing about arbitrage. It hurts buyers in the cheaper market.

&nbsp;

It's interesting to imagine arbitraging physical oil or gas (ie. delivery of many large barrels, which takes up to a month to deliver and can cost around $10 million to delivery and store per oil tanker). I expect arbitrage to be done financially (cash swaps, meaning instead of owning and trading the barrel for cash, they just trade the cash value).
